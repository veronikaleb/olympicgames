Olympic Games

The Olympic Games are the world's only truly global, multi-sport, celebratory athletics competition. With more than 200 countries participating in over 400 events across the Summer and Winter Games, the Olympics are where the world comes to compete, feel inspired, and be together.

Types of Olympics

Summer Olympics

The Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the Olympiad, is a major international multi-sport event held every four years. First held in 1896 in Athens, Greece, the Summer Olympics feature a wide range of sports, including athletics, swimming, gymnastics, and basketball. The games are organized by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) and are hosted by a different city each time.

Winter Olympics

The Winter Olympics, officially known as the Olympic Winter Games, is an international multi-sport event that takes place every four years during the winter season. The first Winter Olympics were held in 1924 in Chamonix, France. These games include winter sports such as skiing, ice skating, and ice hockey.

Paralympics


The Paralympic Games are a series of international multi-sport events for athletes with disabilities. The first official Paralympic Games were held in 1960 in Rome, Italy. The Paralympics are held immediately following the Summer and Winter Olympics, in the same host cities. The games feature sports adapted for athletes with various physical disabilities.

Youth Olympics


The Youth Olympic Games (YOG) are an international multi-sport event for young athletes aged 14 to 18. The first Summer Youth Olympics took place in 2010 in Singapore, and the Winter Youth Olympics were first held in 2012 in Innsbruck, Austria. The games are aimed at promoting Olympic values and encouraging young athletes to pursue excellence.

Our Mission

This website is dedicated to providing in-depth information about the Olympic Games and Paralympic Games, rich history, and the athletes who have made their mark on the global stage. We aim to inspire the next generation of sports enthusiasts and showcase the values of determination, respect, and sportsmanship that the Olympics represent.
